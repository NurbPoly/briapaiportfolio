Suggestive Dichotomies

Graffiti art and high art. Abstract and figurative. Text and images. Afro-Caribbean and American. Basquiat weaved these dichotomies into his work. He had an innate ability to dissect these themes and expose that they may not be so contradictory at all.

Dawn (1960)

Basquiat was born in Brooklyn, NYC, December 22, 1960. His mother, Matilde Basquiat, of Puerto Rican descent was born in Brooklyn. His father, Gerard Basquiat, of Haitian descent was born in Haiti. Basquiat's giftedness was evident from an early age. He could read and write at age 4 and he was fluent in French, Spanish, and English at 11 years of age. His artistic skills alerted his teachers to untapped talent, and he was later enrolled at the exclusive St. Ann's for an arts-centered education.

Day (1970s-1985)

Jean-Michel Basquiat and Al Diaz formed the graffiti duo SAMO. This outlet was Basquiat's first foray into using cryptic messages, haunting poetry, and biting quips targeted at urban society. After years of poverty and homelessness, he sold his first painting for $25,000. His work "The Radiant Child" was published in Artforum and the rest was history. His career took him from New York to Italy and to California. He collaborated with renowned artists such as Andy Warhol and David Bowie.

Dusk (1985-1988)

Basquiat's heroin addiction began to take a toll on his personal life shortly after he appeared on the February 1985 cover for Time Magazine. After Andy Warhol's death on February 22nd, 1987, his addiction and depression further consumed him. Jean-Michel Basquiat died of a heroin overdose at 27 years old.
